# Financial Ratios and Benchmarks

## Savings and Income Ratios

### 50/30/20 Rule
- **50% Needs**: Housing, utilities, groceries, insurance, minimum debt payments, transportation
- **30% Wants**: Dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, shopping, travel, hobbies
- **20% Savings/Debt**: Emergency fund, retirement, extra debt payments, investments

### Savings Rate Benchmarks
| Level | Rate | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Critical | < 5% | Living paycheck to paycheck |
| Below average | 5-10% | National average range (~8%) |
| Good | 10-15% | Building wealth slowly |
| Strong | 15-20% | On track for standard retirement |
| Excellent | 20-30% | Accelerated wealth building |
| FIRE track | 30-50%+ | Financial independence in 10-20 years |

### Income Ratios
- **Gross savings rate**: (Gross Income - Total Expenses) / Gross Income
- **Net savings rate**: (Net Income - Total Expenses) / Net Income
- **Expense ratio**: Total Expenses / Total Income (target: < 80%)

## Liquidity and Emergency Fund

### Emergency Fund Targets
| Situation | Target Months | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Dual income, stable jobs | 3 months | Lower risk of total income loss |
| Single income, stable job | 6 months | Standard recommendation |
| Variable income / freelance | 9-12 months | Income unpredictability |
| Single income + dependents | 6-9 months | Higher responsibility |
| Near retirement (5 years) | 12-24 months | Sequence of returns risk |

### Liquidity Ratio
- **Formula**: Liquid Assets / Monthly Essential Expenses
- **Liquid assets**: Checking + savings + money market (exclude retirement, investment, real estate)
- **Essential expenses**: Housing, utilities, food, insurance, minimum debt payments, transportation
- **Healthy range**: 3.0 - 6.0x

## Debt Ratios

### Debt-to-Income (DTI)
| DTI Range | Assessment | Implications |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| 0-15% | Excellent | Maximum financial flexibility |
| 16-20% | Very Good | Comfortable debt load |
| 21-35% | Manageable | Standard range, room for improvement |
| 36-43% | Elevated | At mortgage qualification threshold |
| 44-50% | High | Financial stress likely |
| 50%+ | Critical | Seek professional debt counseling |

### Housing Ratios
- **Front-end DTI**: Housing costs / Gross income. Target: < 28%
- **Back-end DTI**: All debt payments / Gross income. Target: < 36%
- Housing costs include: mortgage/rent, property tax, insurance, HOA

### Consumer Debt Ratio
- Non-mortgage debt payments / Net income. Target: < 10%
- Includes: credit cards, auto loans, personal loans, student loans

## Retirement Planning Rules

### The 4% Rule (Trinity Study)
- Withdraw 4% of portfolio value in year 1 of retirement
- Adjust for inflation each subsequent year
- High probability of portfolio lasting 30 years
- Conservative alternative: 3.5% for longer retirement or higher safety margin

### The 25x Rule
- **Target portfolio** = 25 x Annual Expenses in retirement
- Example: $60,000/year expenses = $1,500,000 portfolio needed
- For 3.5% withdrawal: multiply by ~29x instead

### Rule of 72
- Years to double money = 72 / Annual return rate
- At 7% return: 72 / 7 = ~10.3 years to double
- At 10% return: 72 / 10 = ~7.2 years to double

### Recommended Contribution Priority
1. 401(k) up to employer match (free money)
2. Pay off high-interest debt (> 7%)
3. Max HSA if eligible ($4,300 individual / $8,550 family in 2025)
4. Max Roth IRA ($7,000 / $8,000 if 50+ in 2025)
5. Max 401(k) ($23,500 / $31,000 if 50+ in 2025)
6. Taxable brokerage account

## Budget Benchmarks by Category

Percentages of take-home (net) pay:

| Category | Target % | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Housing | 25-30% | Rent/mortgage, property tax, insurance, maintenance |
| Transportation | 10-15% | Car payment, insurance, gas, maintenance, transit |
| Food | 10-15% | Groceries 8-10%, dining out 2-5% |
| Insurance & Healthcare | 5-10% | Health, dental, vision premiums + out-of-pocket |
| Utilities | 5-8% | Electric, gas, water, internet, phone |
| Savings & Investments | 15-20% | Emergency fund, retirement, goals |
| Debt Payments | 5-10% | Above minimum payments |
| Personal & Discretionary | 5-10% | Clothing, personal care, gifts |
| Entertainment | 3-5% | Streaming, hobbies, events |
| Education | 0-5% | Student loans, courses, books |

### Red Flags
- Housing > 35% of take-home: housing cost burden
- Food/dining > 20%: likely opportunity to reduce
- Subscriptions > 5%: subscription creep
- No savings allocation: financial vulnerability
