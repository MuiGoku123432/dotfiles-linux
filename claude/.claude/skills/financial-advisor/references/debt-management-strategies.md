# Debt Management Strategies

## Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)

### How It Works
1. List all debts ordered by interest rate, highest first
2. Pay minimum on all debts
3. Direct all extra money to the highest-rate debt
4. When that debt is paid off, roll its payment into the next highest-rate debt
5. Repeat until debt-free

### Strengths
- Minimizes total interest paid
- Mathematically fastest path to debt-free
- Saves the most money over time

### Weaknesses
- If highest-rate debt has a large balance, progress feels slow
- Requires discipline without quick psychological wins
- May take months before first debt is eliminated

### Best For
- Analytical/disciplined individuals
- Large interest rate differences between debts (> 5% spread)
- When the highest-rate debt doesn't have the largest balance

---

## Snowball Method (Psychologically Effective)

### How It Works
1. List all debts ordered by balance, smallest first (ignore interest rates)
2. Pay minimum on all debts
3. Direct all extra money to the smallest-balance debt
4. When that debt is paid off, roll its payment into the next smallest
5. Repeat until debt-free

### Strengths
- Quick wins build momentum and motivation
- Simplifies decision-making (just look at balance)
- Behavioral research shows higher completion rates
- Reduces number of payments to track faster

### Weaknesses
- Costs more in total interest than avalanche
- Mathematically suboptimal
- Can be significantly more expensive if small debts have low rates

### Best For
- People who need motivation and quick wins
- Many small debts that can be eliminated quickly
- When interest rates are similar across debts (< 3% spread)
- History of abandoned debt payoff plans

## Comparison Framework

For each debt, calculate:

| Debt | Balance | Rate | Min Payment | Avalanche Order | Snowball Order |
|------|---------|------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Credit Card A | $3,200 | 24.9% | $96 | 1 | 2 |
| Personal Loan | $8,500 | 12.0% | $180 | 2 | 3 |
| Credit Card B | $1,100 | 19.9% | $35 | 3 | 1 |
| Student Loan | $22,000 | 5.5% | $250 | 4 | 4 |

### Key Comparison Metrics
- **Total interest paid** (avalanche vs snowball)
- **Months to debt-free** (both methods)
- **Dollar savings** (avalanche interest - snowball interest)
- **First debt eliminated** (which method gets a win sooner)
- **Monthly cash flow freed** (as debts are eliminated)

### Recommendation Logic
- If interest savings > $500 AND user seems disciplined: recommend **avalanche**
- If interest savings < $500 OR user needs motivation: recommend **snowball**
- If rates are similar (< 3% spread): recommend **snowball** (similar cost, better psychology)
- Always present both options with specific numbers and let user choose

---

## Hybrid Approach

Consider recommending a hybrid when:
- One small debt can be eliminated in < 2 months (snowball it for the quick win)
- Then switch to avalanche for remaining debts
- Gets psychological momentum while still optimizing interest

## DTI Assessment and Action Thresholds

| DTI Range | Label | Recommended Action |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| 0-15% | Excellent | Focus on investing; debt is well-managed |
| 16-20% | Very Good | Continue current strategy; consider accelerating if rates > 7% |
| 21-35% | Manageable | Standard payoff; prioritize debts above 7% interest |
| 36-43% | Elevated | Aggressive payoff mode; reduce discretionary spending 10-15% |
| 44-50% | High Stress | Cut discretionary to minimum; consider debt consolidation |
| 50%+ | Critical | Seek credit counseling; consider hardship programs |

## Refinancing and Consolidation Triggers

### Credit Card Balance Transfer
- **Trigger**: Balance > $3,000 at rate > 18%
- **Action**: Apply for 0% intro APR balance transfer card (typically 15-21 months)
- **Watch for**: Transfer fee (typically 3-5%), rate after intro period
- **Math**: Transfer fee cost vs interest savings over intro period

### Personal Loan Consolidation
- **Trigger**: Multiple debts with blended rate > 15%
- **Action**: Consolidate into fixed-rate personal loan (typically 7-15%)
- **Watch for**: Origination fees, longer term increasing total interest
- **Math**: Monthly payment reduction AND total interest comparison

### Mortgage Refinance
- **Trigger**: Current rate > market rate by 0.75%+ AND staying 5+ years
- **Action**: Refinance to lower rate
- **Watch for**: Closing costs (2-5% of loan), break-even period
- **Math**: Monthly savings x months remaining - closing costs = net benefit

### Student Loan Refinance
- **Trigger**: Federal loans with rate > 6% AND stable income AND no need for IDR/PSLF
- **Action**: Refinance with private lender for lower rate
- **Warning**: Loses federal protections (IDR, forgiveness, forbearance)
- **Math**: Interest savings vs value of federal protections

### Auto Loan Refinance
- **Trigger**: Rate > current market by 2%+ AND good credit score
- **Action**: Refinance with credit union or online lender
- **Watch for**: Remaining loan term, vehicle age/value requirements

## When to Seek Professional Help

Recommend professional credit counseling or debt management when:
- DTI > 50% and unable to make minimum payments
- Being contacted by collections agencies
- Considering bankruptcy
- Debt is causing significant mental health impact
- Multiple missed payments in past 6 months

Resources:
- National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC): nfcc.org
- Note: Avoid for-profit debt settlement companies
