# Investment Allocation Frameworks

## Age-Based Allocation Rules of Thumb

| Approach | Bond Allocation | Stock Allocation | Use Case |
|----------|----------------|------------------|----------|
| Conservative | Age% bonds | (100 - Age)% stocks | Risk-averse, near retirement |
| Moderate | (Age - 10)% bonds | (110 - Age)% stocks | Balanced approach |
| Aggressive | (Age - 20)% bonds | (120 - Age)% stocks | Long time horizon, high risk tolerance |

Example for a 40-year-old:
- Conservative: 40% bonds, 60% stocks
- Moderate: 30% bonds, 70% stocks
- Aggressive: 20% bonds, 80% stocks

## Diversification Targets

### Geographic Diversification
- **US Stocks**: 60-70% of equity allocation
- **International Developed**: 20-30% of equity allocation
- **Emerging Markets**: 5-15% of equity allocation
- Rationale: US is ~60% of world market cap, but home bias is common

### Market Cap Diversification
- **Large Cap**: 60-70% of stock allocation
- **Mid Cap**: 15-20% of stock allocation
- **Small Cap**: 10-15% of stock allocation
- Small/mid caps have historically higher returns but higher volatility

### Bond Diversification
- **US Government**: 30-40% of bond allocation (safety)
- **Corporate Investment Grade**: 25-35% (higher yield)
- **TIPS**: 10-20% (inflation protection)
- **International Bonds**: 10-20% (diversification)
- **High Yield**: 0-10% (income, higher risk)

### Alternative Assets (Optional, Advanced)
- **REITs**: 0-10% of total portfolio
- **Commodities**: 0-5%
- **Other**: crypto, private equity — generally < 5% for speculative allocation

## Concentration Risk Limits

| Threshold | Assessment | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Single holding > 5% | Monitor | Acceptable for core holdings |
| Single holding > 10% | Elevated | Plan gradual diversification |
| Single holding > 15% | High | Active reduction recommended |
| Single holding > 25% | Critical | Immediate diversification needed |
| Single sector > 30% | Elevated | Review sector exposure |
| No international exposure | Gap | Add international allocation |

### Common Concentration Issues
- Company stock from employer (RSUs, ESPP) — often > 20%
- Single tech stock from early investment
- Overlapping funds (multiple S&P 500 funds = concentration, not diversification)
- All bonds in one type (e.g., only US Treasuries)

## Rebalancing Strategies

### Time-Based
- **Annual**: Simple, tax-efficient, sufficient for most investors
- **Semi-annual**: Moderate frequency, good balance
- **Quarterly**: More precise, but more transactions/taxes

### Threshold-Based
- Rebalance when any asset class drifts > 5% from target allocation
- Example: If target is 70% stocks and current is 76%, rebalance
- More responsive to market moves than time-based

### Preferred Rebalancing Methods (Tax Efficiency)
1. **Direct new contributions**: Invest new money into underweight asset classes
2. **Redirect dividends**: Point dividend reinvestment to underweight classes
3. **Rebalance in tax-advantaged accounts**: No tax consequences in 401k/IRA
4. **Tax-loss harvest**: Sell overweight losers in taxable, buy underweight
5. **Sell in taxable** (last resort): Triggers capital gains taxes

## Tax-Location Optimization

### What to Hold Where

| Account Type | Best For | Why |
|-------------|----------|-----|
| **401(k) / Traditional IRA** | Bonds, REITs, high-turnover funds | Tax-inefficient assets; defer taxes on interest income |
| **Roth IRA** | Highest growth potential (small cap, emerging markets) | Tax-free growth; maximize the tax-free benefit |
| **Taxable Brokerage** | Tax-efficient index funds, municipal bonds, long-term holds | Low turnover = fewer taxable events; qualified dividends at lower rate |
| **HSA** | Highest growth assets (if not using for current expenses) | Triple tax benefit: deductible, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawal for medical |

### Tax-Location Priority
1. Place tax-inefficient assets (bonds, REITs) in tax-deferred accounts first
2. Place highest-growth assets in Roth accounts
3. Place tax-efficient equity index funds in taxable accounts
4. Municipal bonds in taxable accounts (tax-exempt interest)

## Common Investment Mistakes to Flag

1. **Cash drag**: Holding > 10% cash in investment accounts (opportunity cost)
2. **Home bias**: < 15% international in equity allocation
3. **Recency bias**: Chasing recent performance (buying high)
4. **Over-diversification**: 20+ funds with significant overlap
5. **Under-diversification**: < 5 holdings or single sector concentration
6. **Wrong account type**: Bonds in Roth, growth stocks in Traditional
7. **High fees**: Expense ratios > 0.50% for passive strategies
8. **No rebalancing**: Never rebalanced since initial allocation
9. **Market timing**: Large cash positions waiting to "buy the dip"
10. **Ignoring tax-advantaged space**: Not maxing 401k match = leaving free money
